This is a very distinct aesthetic often found in high-quality computer science research papers, technical documentation, and system design whitepapers.

If you are building software to replicate this, you are aiming for a style I would call **"Academic Modern"** or **"Publication-Ready Technical."**

Here is the breakdown of the vibe, colors, and clarity vision to help you program the rendering engine.

### 1. The Vibe: "Authoritative & Structural"
The emotional feel of this diagram is **reliable, organized, and scientifically rigorous**. It doesn't look like a casual whiteboard sketch; it looks like a "final draft" intended for print or a formal presentation.
* **Keywords:** Precision, Hierarchy, Modular, Clean, Vector-based.
* **The Vision:** The goal is to reduce cognitive load. The viewer should immediately understand which boxes belong together (grouping) and the direction of data flow without being distracted by flashy gradients or neon colors.

### 2. Color Palette: "Functional Pastels"
The color story is crucial here. It avoids high-saturation primary colors. Instead, it uses a **muted, pastel palette** where color indicates *function* rather than decoration.

* **Background:** Pure White (`#FFFFFF`).
* **Strokes/Outlines:** Dark Charcoal or Soft Black (`#333333`). Not pure black, which can look harsh.
* **Fill Colors (The "Pastel Logic"):**
    * **Lavender/Soft Purple:** Used here for Memory/AI Logic (`#E6E0EC` to `#D1C4E9`).
    * **Pale Blue:** Used for the Primary Orchestrator/Core System (`#D8E5F3` to `#BBDEFB`).
    * **Soft Peach/Orange:** Used for Message Brokers/Transit (`#FFE0B2` or `#FFCCBC`).
    * **Neutral Grey:** Used for External entities (User, Database, API) (`#F5F5F5` or `#EEEEEE`).
* **Constraint:** If your software generates these, ensure the text contrast is high. Dark text on light pastel backgrounds is the key signature here.

### 3. Component Design: "The Card Metaphor"
The clarity comes from how the nodes (boxes) are rendered. They feel like physical cards placed on a surface.

* **Shape:** Rectangles with **Soft Rounded Corners**.
    * *Tech Spec:* `border-radius: 6px` to `8px`.
* **Depth:** A very subtle **Drop Shadow**. It lifts the elements off the page just enough to create separation.
    * *Tech Spec:* `box-shadow: 2px 2px 5px rgba(0,0,0,0.1)`.
* **Borders:** Thin, consistent line weights.
    * *Tech Spec:* `stroke-width: 1px` or `1.5px`.
* **Containers (Sub-systems):** Notice the "Proactive Memory System" and "Specialized Agents" areas. These are grouped by **Dashed Lines** with a transparent or very faint gray fill. This is critical for showing "parent-child" relationships.

### 4. Typography: "The Serif/Sans-Serif Hybrid"
This is a defining feature of the "Academic" style.

* **The Title:** The top header ("VOS: Multi-Agent...") is likely a **Serif font** (like *Times New Roman*, *Georgia*, or *Computer Modern*). This signals "Research Paper."
* **The Labels:** The text inside the boxes is a clean **Sans-Serif** (like *Helvetica*, *Arial*, or *Roboto*). This signals "User Interface" or "Modern Tech."
* **Hierarchy:** Inside the boxes, the primary label is **Bold**, and the secondary label (tech stack details like "FastAPI" or "gemini-3-pro") is **Regular** and slightly smaller.

### 5. Connectivity: "Orthogonal Routing"
The arrows and lines (edges) are strict and disciplined.

* **Routing:** Orthogonal. Lines move strictly horizontal or vertical with 90-degree turns. No organic curves or diagonal shortcuts.
* **Line Styles:**
    * **Solid:** Direct data flow.
    * **Dashed:** Asynchronous flow, feedback loops, or secondary signals (like the "flash-lite" or "results" lines).
* **Arrowheads:** Small, filled triangles. Sharp and precise.
